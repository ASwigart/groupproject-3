AFFIRMATIVE AND DEFENSIVE ASYLUM IN THE US

Asylum seekers are those who meet the criteria for refugee status but apply from within the United States, or at ports of entry,
after arriving under a different status. Asylum seekers follow a different protocol than those applying for refugee status.

Year:

It was not until after the fall of South Vietnam to communist forces in 1975, when the United States began taking in hundreds of
thousands of Southeast Asian refugees, that Congress established a more standardized system. As humanitarian crises around
the world grew, a ceiling of 110,000 refugees was set under President Obama. Throughout his presidency, President Trump
continued to lower the cap until it was its absolute lowest at 15,000 in fiscal year 2021.
